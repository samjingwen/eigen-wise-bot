\begin{minipage}{0.65\linewidth}
Suppose $V$ is an $n$-dimensional complex vector space and $T$ is a linear operator on $V$ such that
$\null(T^{n-3}) \neq \null(T^{n-2})$. What can we say about the number of distinct eigenvalues of $T$?
\end{minipage}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item It is exactly $3$.
  \item It is exactly $n-3$.
  \item It is at most $3$.
  \item It is at least $3$.
\end{enumerate}
